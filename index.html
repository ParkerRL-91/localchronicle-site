Local Chronicle is a Mac app that records, transcribes, and summarizes meetings entirely on your device. FAQ

Common questions

Everything you need to know before downloading.

Does Local Chronicle upload my audio or transcripts to the cloud?
No. Local Chronicle runs entirely on your Mac. Your audio, transcripts, and AI summaries never leave your device. There are no servers, no cloud storage, and no third-party data processing.
Do I need a subscription?
No. Local Chronicle is a one-time $9.99 purchase. There are no monthly fees, no annual renewals, and no feature paywalls. You buy it once and own it forever.
Do I need an account or login?
No account is required. Download the app, pay once, and start recording. No email, no password, no sign-up flow.
Which meeting apps does it work with?
Local Chronicle works with any app that plays audio through your Mac — Zoom, Google Meet, Microsoft Teams, Webex, FaceTime, and in-person meetings recorded through your microphone.
How does it transcribe without the cloud?
Local Chronicle uses on-device speech recognition powered by WhisperKit, an optimized version of OpenAI's Whisper model that runs locally on Apple Silicon. No internet connection is needed for transcription.
How is this different from Otter.ai or Granola?
Otter.ai and Granola upload your audio to cloud servers and require subscriptions. Local Chronicle keeps everything on your Mac, requires no account, and costs $9.99 one time. For privacy-sensitive work — legal, medical, financial — there is no comparison.
Does it work without an internet connection?
Yes. Transcription and AI summarization work fully offline. An internet connection is only needed to download the app initially and to pull the AI model on first setup.
What happens to my data if the company shuts down?
Because everything runs on your Mac, there is no server to shut down. Your recordings and transcripts are stored locally and remain accessible regardless of what happens to the product.
